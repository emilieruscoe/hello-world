With the MTA in decline, cycling is on the rise in New York

By Emilie Ruscoe

In a city where many rely on a public transit system that officials say is in a state of emergency, cycling is booming.

The chart above shows how cycling numbers have increased at ten sites in Manhattan over the course of the past decade.

"Safety innovations like pedestrian islands, retimed traffic signals, and a lower speed limit have saved countless New Yorkers' lives and made walking and cycling in New York City easier, safer, and more fun," says Paul Steely White, the Executive Director of cycling advocacy organization Transportation Alternatives. "Hundreds of miles of new bike lanes, including over 90 miles of protected bike lanes, are used by hundreds of thousands of cyclists per year."

New Yorkers may be opting for warmer commutes for the winter, but trends indicate that come spring, they'll be back on the roads in full force.
